# New York Grid Electricity Demand & Temperature Analysis
An exploratory data analysis (EDA) and predictive modeling project evaluating the impact of ambient temperature fluctuations on regional power grid electricity demand.

## Project Overview
In this project, I perform an exploratory data analysis (EDA) to investigate the relationship between temperature and electricity demand in New York. The datasets consist of time-series measurements of ambient temperature (°C) and electricity demand (MW). These variables are continuous and are expected to exhibit a relationship due to the increased use of cooling systems during periods of high temperature.

By quantifying how cooling loads drive peak electricity demand, this analysis provides insights relevant to smart utility infrastructure, grid capacity planning, and resilient energy systems engineering.
